## Anchor Tenant

A large tenant that occupies the biggest portions of a shopping center's retail space and serves as the primary driver of foot traffic to the property. Anchor tenants are typically positioned at the ends of shopping centers and include major department stores, grocery stores, or big box retailers that attract customers who then shop at smaller inline stores.

**Categories**: Property Management, Leasing, Retail Strategy  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: Anchor Store, Key Tenant  
**Synonyms**: Major Tenant, Draw Tenant  
**Tags**: anchor, tenant, traffic, retail, shopping-center

---

## Additional Rent

Any costs for a tenant in addition to the minimum rent or base rent outlined in a lease agreement. Additional rent typically includes operating expenses, common area maintenance charges, property taxes, insurance premiums, and utilities that are passed through to tenants based on their proportionate share of the property.

**Categories**: Leasing, Finance, Property Management  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: Pass-Through Charges, Operating Expense Charges  
**Synonyms**: Extra Rent, Expense Charges  
**Tags**: rent, expenses, operating-costs, lease, tenant

---

## Base Rent

The minimum fixed rent amount a tenant pays to occupy commercial space, typically expressed as a dollar amount per square foot annually or monthly. Base rent does not include additional charges such as operating expenses, taxes, or percentage rent, and serves as the foundation rental payment before any additional costs are applied.

**Categories**: Leasing, Finance, Property Management  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: Minimum Rent, Fixed Minimum Rent, Base Minimum Rent  
**Synonyms**: Minimum Rent, Fixed Rent  
**Tags**: rent, base, minimum, lease, fixed

---

## Breakpoint

In percentage rent lease structures, the sales threshold at which tenants begin paying additional rent based on a percentage of gross sales above this amount. Breakpoints can be natural (calculated by dividing base rent by percentage rate) or artificial (negotiated amount), and if never met, the tenant pays only the minimum base rent.

**Categories**: Leasing, Finance, Retail Strategy  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: Natural Breakpoint, Artificial Breakpoint  
**Synonyms**: Sales Threshold, Percentage Rent Trigger  
**Tags**: percentage-rent, sales, threshold, retail, lease

---

## CAM Charges

Common Area Maintenance charges are fees tenants pay for their proportionate share of maintaining and operating shared spaces in a commercial property. CAM charges typically cover expenses for parking lots, sidewalks, landscaping, janitorial services, security, snow removal, and general upkeep of areas used by all tenants.

**Categories**: Property Management, Finance, Operations  
**Abbreviations**: CAM  
**Variations**: Common Area Maintenance, Common Area Charges  
**Synonyms**: Shared Area Costs, Maintenance Charges  
**Tags**: cam, maintenance, common-area, expenses, shared

---

## Co-Tenancy Clause

A lease provision that protects tenants by linking their lease obligations to the occupancy status of key anchor tenants or overall occupancy levels within a shopping center. If specified conditions are not met, such as anchor tenant closure or occupancy dropping below a threshold, tenants may receive rent reductions, lease suspension, or termination rights.

**Categories**: Leasing, Risk Management, Retail Strategy  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: Co-Tenancy Provision, Opening Co-Tenancy, Operating Co-Tenancy  
**Synonyms**: Occupancy Protection Clause, Tenant Protection Provision  
**Tags**: co-tenancy, protection, occupancy, anchor, clause

---

## Common Area

Shared spaces within a commercial property that are used by all tenants and their customers, including lobbies, hallways, restrooms, parking lots, sidewalks, and landscaped areas. Common areas are maintained through CAM charges and are factored into rentable square footage calculations for determining tenant rent allocations.

**Categories**: Property Management, Operations, Leasing  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: Shared Areas, Public Areas  
**Synonyms**: Shared Space, Public Space  
**Tags**: common-area, shared, space, maintenance, property

---

## Delivery Condition

The specific state in which a landlord must deliver retail space to a tenant, ranging from as-is condition to turnkey ready. Common delivery conditions include vanilla shell (finished with basic systems), gray shell (unfinished space), white box (basic finished space), or turnkey (fully completed and ready for occupancy).

**Categories**: Leasing, Property Management, Development  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: As-Is, Vanilla Shell, Gray Shell, White Box, Turnkey  
**Synonyms**: Space Condition, Delivery Standard  
**Tags**: delivery, condition, shell, turnkey, space

---

## Effective Rent

The total actual rent paid by a tenant including minimum rent, additional rent, percentage rent, and any other charges, minus any concessions such as free rent periods or tenant improvement allowances. Effective rent provides the true cost of occupancy and helps compare different lease proposals on an equivalent basis.

**Categories**: Finance, Leasing, Property Management  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: True Rent, Net Effective Rent  
**Synonyms**: Actual Rent, Total Occupancy Cost  
**Tags**: effective-rent, total-cost, net-rent, comparison, lease

---

## Escalation Clause

A lease provision that allows landlords to increase rent or other charges over the lease term, typically tied to increases in operating expenses, inflation indices, or predetermined fixed amounts. Escalations help landlords maintain income levels despite rising operational costs and are usually applied annually on lease anniversary dates.

**Categories**: Leasing, Finance, Property Management  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: Rent Escalation, Annual Increase, CPI Adjustment  
**Synonyms**: Rent Increase Provision, Adjustment Clause  
**Tags**: escalation, increase, inflation, adjustment, lease

---

## Exclusivity Provision

A lease clause that prohibits landlords from leasing space in the same property to competing businesses or specific categories of retailers. Exclusivity provisions protect tenants from direct competition within the shopping center and are particularly important for specialty retailers or restaurants seeking to maintain market position.

**Categories**: Leasing, Retail Strategy, Risk Management  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: Non-Competition Clause, Exclusive Use Provision  
**Synonyms**: Competition Protection, Exclusive Rights  
**Tags**: exclusivity, competition, protection, retail, clause

---

## Gross Leasable Area

The total floor space in a commercial property that can be rented to tenants, measured from the outside walls and including both tenant-specific areas and common areas. GLA is used to calculate rental rates, determine tenant proportionate shares, and assess property performance metrics for shopping centers and retail properties.

**Categories**: Property Management, Leasing, Finance  
**Abbreviations**: GLA  
**Variations**: Total Leasable Area, Rentable Area  
**Synonyms**: Leasable Space, Rental Area  
**Tags**: gla, area, leasable, measurement, space

---

## Gross Lease

A lease structure where tenants pay a single amount that covers base rent and all operating expenses including taxes, insurance, maintenance, and utilities. Under a gross lease, landlords are responsible for all property operating costs, providing tenants with predictable monthly payments but typically at higher rental rates to cover landlord risk.

**Categories**: Leasing, Finance, Property Management  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: Full Service Lease, All-Inclusive Lease  
**Synonyms**: Full Service Gross, Inclusive Lease  
**Tags**: gross-lease, full-service, inclusive, expenses, lease-type

---

## Inline Store

Traditional retail spaces located along the corridors or main walkways of shopping centers and malls, positioned between anchor tenants. Inline stores are typically smaller specialty retailers that benefit from the foot traffic generated by anchor tenants and represent the majority of tenant spaces in most shopping centers.

**Categories**: Retail Strategy, Property Management, Leasing  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: Shop Space, Corridor Store  
**Synonyms**: Mall Shop, Corridor Tenant  
**Tags**: inline, store, corridor, specialty, retail

---

## Kiosk

A small, often temporary retail unit typically located in common areas or mall corridors, offering a 360-degree selling environment with high visibility and foot traffic. Kiosks provide an affordable entry point for retailers to test markets or seasonal concepts and generate additional revenue for property owners from previously unused space.

**Categories**: Retail Strategy, Leasing, Property Management  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: Retail Merchandising Unit, Cart, Pushcart  
**Synonyms**: RMU, Retail Cart  
**Tags**: kiosk, temporary, cart, rmu, retail

---

## Load Factor

The ratio of rentable square footage to usable square footage, representing the percentage of common area space allocated to tenants. Load factor helps tenants evaluate different properties by understanding how much common area cost is included in their rent, with typical retail load factors ranging from 10% to 25%.

**Categories**: Property Management, Finance, Leasing  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: Add-On Factor, Loss Factor  
**Synonyms**: Common Area Factor, Rentable-to-Usable Ratio  
**Tags**: load-factor, ratio, common-area, rentable, usable

---

## Merchants Association

A formal organization of tenants within a shopping center that coordinates joint marketing efforts, community events, and common interests. Merchants associations are typically structured as non-profit corporations that collect dues from member tenants to fund promotional activities, seasonal decorations, and events that drive customer traffic to the property.

**Categories**: Marketing, Operations, Business Strategy  
**Abbreviations**: MA  
**Variations**: Tenant Association, Shopping Center Association  
**Synonyms**: Retailer Association, Tenant Group  
**Tags**: merchants, association, marketing, tenants, promotion

---

## Modified Gross Lease

A lease structure where landlords and tenants share certain operating expenses, with tenants paying base rent plus some but not all property operating costs. Common arrangements include tenants paying utilities and janitorial while landlords cover taxes and insurance, providing a middle ground between gross and net lease structures.

**Categories**: Leasing, Finance, Property Management  
**Abbreviations**: MG  
**Variations**: Modified Net Lease, Semi-Gross Lease  
**Synonyms**: Shared Expense Lease, Partial Net Lease  
**Tags**: modified-gross, shared-expenses, lease-type, hybrid, costs

---

## Net Lease

A lease structure where tenants pay base rent plus their proportionate share of property operating expenses such as taxes, insurance, and maintenance. Net leases transfer operating cost risks to tenants while providing landlords with more predictable income, with variations including single net, double net, and triple net arrangements.

**Categories**: Leasing, Finance, Property Management  
**Abbreviations**: N  
**Variations**: Single Net, Double Net, Triple Net  
**Synonyms**: Net Rent Lease, Expense Pass-Through Lease  
**Tags**: net-lease, expenses, pass-through, operating-costs, lease-type

---

## Occupancy Rate

The percentage of total leasable space in a property that is currently occupied by paying tenants, calculated as occupied square footage divided by total leasable square footage. Occupancy rate is a key performance indicator for retail properties, with higher rates indicating successful leasing and property management.

**Categories**: Property Management, Finance, Operations  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: Leased Percentage, Tenant Occupancy  
**Synonyms**: Lease Rate, Fill Rate  
**Tags**: occupancy, rate, performance, leased, percentage

---

## Percentage Rent

Additional rent paid beyond base rent based on a percentage of tenant gross sales above a specified breakpoint. Percentage rent is common in retail leases and allows landlords to participate in tenant success while providing tenants with lower base rents, typically ranging from 1% to 7% of sales depending on the business type.

**Categories**: Leasing, Finance, Retail Strategy  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: Overage Rent, Sales-Based Rent  
**Synonyms**: Sales Rent, Revenue Sharing  
**Tags**: percentage-rent, sales, overage, retail, revenue

---

## Pop-Up Store

A temporary retail concept that occupies space for a limited time period, typically ranging from a few days to several months. Pop-up stores allow retailers to test new markets, launch products, or create seasonal presence while providing property owners with income from otherwise vacant spaces and increased foot traffic.

**Categories**: Retail Strategy, Leasing, Marketing  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: Temporary Store, Short-Term Retail  
**Synonyms**: Temporary Retail, Short-Term Lease  
**Tags**: pop-up, temporary, short-term, retail, concept

---

## Rentable Square Footage

The total area for which tenants pay rent, including both usable space and a proportionate share of common areas such as lobbies, restrooms, and corridors. Rentable square footage is the standard measurement used to calculate rental rates and is typically larger than usable square footage due to the inclusion of shared spaces.

**Categories**: Property Management, Leasing, Finance  
**Abbreviations**: RSF  
**Variations**: Rentable Area, Gross Rentable Area  
**Synonyms**: Billable Area, Rental Area  
**Tags**: rentable, square-footage, rsf, area, measurement

---

## Tenant Improvement Allowance

A monetary contribution provided by landlords to tenants for customizing or improving leased space to meet specific business needs. Tenant improvement allowances are typically calculated per square foot and help attract tenants by reducing their upfront costs, with any excess costs above the allowance paid by the tenant.

**Categories**: Leasing, Finance, Development  
**Abbreviations**: TI, TIA  
**Variations**: Build-Out Allowance, Improvement Allowance  
**Synonyms**: Construction Allowance, Fit-Out Allowance  
**Tags**: tenant-improvement, allowance, build-out, construction, fit-out

---

## Tenant Mix

The strategic combination and arrangement of different types of retailers and service providers within a shopping center to maximize foot traffic, sales, and overall property performance. Effective tenant mix creates synergies between complementary businesses and provides customers with a comprehensive shopping and service experience.

**Categories**: Retail Strategy, Property Management, Marketing  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: Retailer Mix, Store Mix  
**Synonyms**: Merchant Mix, Retail Composition  
**Tags**: tenant-mix, strategy, synergy, retail, composition

---

## Triple Net Lease

A lease structure where tenants pay base rent plus their proportionate share of all three major property expenses: real estate taxes, building insurance, and common area maintenance. Triple net leases transfer most property operating risks to tenants while providing landlords with predictable net income streams.

**Categories**: Leasing, Finance, Property Management  
**Abbreviations**: NNN, Triple Net  
**Variations**: Net-Net-Net Lease, Absolute Net Lease  
**Synonyms**: NNN Lease, Full Net Lease  
**Tags**: triple-net, nnn, taxes, insurance, maintenance

---

## Usable Square Footage

The area within leased premises that tenants can actually use for their business operations, measured from the inside face of exterior walls and excluding common areas. Usable square footage represents the space tenants physically occupy and use, as distinct from rentable square footage which includes common area allocations.

**Categories**: Property Management, Leasing, Operations  
**Abbreviations**: USF  
**Variations**: Usable Area, Net Usable Area  
**Synonyms**: Occupied Area, Actual Use Area  
**Tags**: usable, square-footage, usf, area, occupied

---

## Vacancy Rate

The percentage of total leasable space that remains unoccupied and available for lease, calculated as vacant square footage divided by total leasable square footage. Vacancy rate is the inverse of occupancy rate and serves as a key indicator of market conditions, property performance, and leasing effectiveness.

**Categories**: Property Management, Finance, Operations  
**Abbreviations**: N/A  
**Variations**: Vacant Percentage, Unleased Rate  
**Synonyms**: Empty Rate, Available Space Percentage  
**Tags**: vacancy, rate, unleased, available, percentage
